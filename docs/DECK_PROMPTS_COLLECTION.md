## Italian Common Phrases (Learning Deck)

**Prompt:**

```
Create a deck of 20 essential Italian phrases for beginners learning the language.

FRONT OF CARD:
- Title: The Italian phrase (in Italian)
- Subtitle: English translation
- Description: A practical example of when/how to use this phrase (2-3 sentences), including cultural context if relevant
- Traits (only ONE public):
  * Public: Formality level or usage context (e.g., "Informal", "Formal greeting", "Restaurant essential")
  * Private: Grammar note (e.g., "Uses subjunctive", "Idiomatic expression")
  * Private: Difficulty level or learning tip

BACK OF CARD (STATS):
Use stats to break down the grammar/structure:
- Word count: [number]
- Difficulty: [1-10]
- Formality: [1-10, where 1=very casual, 10=very formal]

BACK OF CARD (DESCRIPTION):
Provide a detailed grammatical explanation:
- Why is this phrase constructed this way?
- What grammatical concepts does it use? (e.g., "uses genitive case", "feminine plural", "conditional tense")
- Literal vs. idiomatic translation if different
- Common mistakes learners make with this phrase
- Related phrases or variations

PHRASE SELECTION:
Include a mix of:
- Greetings and politeness (Buongiorno, Grazie, Prego, etc.)
- Essential questions (Dov'Ã¨...?, Quanto costa?, Parla inglese?)
- Survival phrases (Non capisco, Mi puÃ² aiutare?)
- Restaurant/food phrases (Il conto per favore, Vorrei...)
- Social phrases (Come stai?, Molto piacere)
- Useful expressions (Non c'Ã¨ problema, Mi dispiace)

THEME: Educational, practical Italian language learning
ART STYLE: Modern, clean - consider using Italian cultural imagery (food, architecture, maps) in a minimalist style
```

## Orkish Phrases (Fantasy Language)

**Prompt:**

```
Create a deck of 20 Orkish phrases for a fantasy RPG setting - brutal, guttural, and practical.

FRONT OF CARD:
- Title: The Orkish phrase (invent a guttural, consonant-heavy phrase)
- Subtitle: English translation
- Description: Context and usage - when would an Ork say this? Include flavor about Ork culture, values, and communication style (2-3 sentences)
- Traits (only ONE public):
  * Public: Context (e.g., "Battle cry", "Insult", "Command", "Greeting")
  * Private: Tone (e.g., "Threatening", "Respectful to superior", "Dismissive")
  * Private: Social rank required (e.g., "Boss only", "Any warrior", "Shaman phrase")

BACK OF CARD (STATS):
- Aggression Level: [1-10]
- Formality: [1-10, where 1=grunt, 10=ritual speech]
- Volume Usually Spoken At: [1-10, where 10=SHOUTING]

BACK OF CARD (DESCRIPTION):
Provide linguistic and cultural notes:
- Etymology or word breakdown if relevant
- Cultural context: What does this phrase reveal about Ork society?
- When NOT to use this phrase (consequences)
- Regional variations or alternative phrasings
- Related phrases or insults

PHRASE SELECTION:
Include a mix of:
- Battle cries and war phrases
- Commands and military orders
- Insults and challenges
- Greetings (likely aggressive)
- Food and drink demands
- Claims of dominance or strength
- Tribal/clan references
- Expressions of respect (to stronger Orks)

LINGUISTICS:
- Use harsh consonants: K, G, R, Z, GH, KH
- Minimal vowels, lots of guttural sounds
- Short, punchy phrases (Orks don't waste words)
- Consider compound words (e.g., "skull-basher", "gut-ripper")

THEME: Fantasy, Ork culture, brutal and direct communication
ART STYLE: Inked - bold, tattoo-inspired Orkish imagery with neutral backgrounds
```

## Hobbit Inn Menu Items

**Prompt:**

```
Create a deck of 20 delicious dishes and drinks served at a cozy Hobbit inn in the Shire.

FRONT OF CARD:
- Title: Name of the dish/drink (whimsical, Hobbit-style names)
- Subtitle: Category (e.g., "Hearty Breakfast", "Afternoon Tea Treat", "Evening Ale", "Second Breakfast Staple")
- Description: Mouthwatering description of the dish - ingredients, preparation, taste, aroma, and why Hobbits love it (3-4 sentences). Make it feel warm, comforting, and abundant.
- Traits (only ONE public):
  * Public: Best enjoyed at... (e.g., "Second Breakfast", "Elevenses", "Luncheon", "Afternoon Tea", "Dinner", "Supper")
  * Private: Specialty of... (e.g., "Green Dragon Inn", "Prancing Pony", "Ivy Bush")
  * Private: Pairs well with... (another dish or drink)

BACK OF CARD (STATS):
For ingredients, use quantities:
- Main ingredient (quantity)
- Secondary ingredient (quantity)
- Key seasoning/element
- Servings: [number]
OR for general stats:
- Heartiness: [1-10]
- Sweetness: [1-10, for desserts/drinks]
- Prep Time (minutes): [actual number]

BACK OF CARD (DESCRIPTION):
Include:
- Full ingredient list
- Brief preparation notes
- Serving suggestions
- Historical or cultural notes (is this a traditional Shire recipe? A family secret?)
- Anecdotes about Hobbits enjoying this dish
- Seasonal availability or special occasions when it's served

DISH SELECTION:
Include a mix of:
- Breakfast items (eggs, bacon, mushrooms, sausages, toast, pastries)
- Baked goods (pies, cakes, scones, biscuits, seed-cakes)
- Hearty mains (roasts, stews, meat pies, fish dishes)
- Side dishes (roasted vegetables, potatoes prepared various ways)
- Drinks (ales, ciders, tea, coffee, cordials)
- Desserts (tarts, puddings, jellies, honeyed treats)

NAMING STYLE:
- Simple, rustic, warm names
- Often includes the main ingredient
- Sometimes references Shire locations or Hobbit families
- Examples: "Bywater Beef Stew", "Tookish Honey Cake", "Green Dragon's Best Brown Ale"

THEME: Fantasy, cozy, wholesome, abundant Hobbit hospitality
ART STYLE: Classic (watercolor sketch) - warm, inviting food illustrations with rustic, homey feel
```

## Fantasy Taverns & Their Signs

**Prompt:**

```
Create a deck of 20 fantasy taverns, each with a distinctive name, atmosphere, and iconic hanging sign.

FRONT OF CARD:
- Title: Tavern name (evocative and memorable)
- Subtitle: Location/Region (e.g., "Harbor District, Port City", "King's Road Crossroads", "Dwarven Quarter")
- Description: Vivid description of the tavern's atmosphere, clientele, reputation, and what makes it unique (3-4 sentences). Include sensory details: sounds, smells, lighting, crowd.
- Traits (only ONE public):
  * Public: Reputation (e.g., "Rough crowd", "Respectable establishment", "Adventurer hangout", "Merchant favorite")
  * Private: Secret or notable feature (e.g., "Underground tunnel access", "Front for thieves guild", "Owner is retired adventurer")
  * Private: Best known for... (e.g., "Excellent ale", "Live music", "Cheap rooms", "Gambling den")

BACK OF CARD (STATS):
- Danger Level: [1-10, where 1=safe, 10=deadly]
- Atmosphere: [1-10, where 1=quiet, 10=rowdy]
- Price Range: [1-10, where 1=cheap, 10=expensive]
- Room Quality: [1-10]

BACK OF CARD (DESCRIPTION):
Include:
- History of the establishment (how long it's been around, founded by whom)
- The hanging sign: detailed description of what it looks like (important for the artwork!)
- Notable NPCs (owner, bartender, regulars)
- Menu specialties
- Room rates and availability
- Recent events or rumors associated with the tavern
- Adventure hooks (what might happen here?)

TAVERN TYPES:
Include variety:
- Rough dockside taverns
- Respectable merchant inns
- Adventurer gathering spots
- Seedy criminal hideouts
- Cozy rural roadside inns
- Elegant city establishments
- Dwarven ale halls
- Elven wine gardens
- Themed establishments (The Dragon's Flagon, The Rusty Sword)

SIGN DESCRIPTIONS:
Be specific about the hanging sign imagery:
- Animal imagery (prancing pony, sleeping dragon, golden griffin)
- Object imagery (rusty sword, broken wheel, silver tankard)
- Action scenes (crossed swords, leaping fish, dancing bear)
- Colors and condition (faded, freshly painted, ornate, simple)
- This is what will be illustrated!

THEME: Fantasy, D&D-style taverns, diverse and atmospheric
ART STYLE: Consider Inked style for the tavern signs - iconic, tattoo-worthy imagery with neutral backgrounds
```

## Drow Poisons & Antidotes

**Prompt:**

```
Create a deck of 20 deadly Drow poisons, each with its effects and antidote.

FRONT OF CARD:
- Title: Poison name (in Common or Drow/Undercommon if appropriate - dark, sinister names)
- Subtitle: Poison type (e.g., "Contact Poison", "Ingested Toxin", "Injury Poison - Blade Coating", "Inhaled Spore")
- Description: Detailed description of the poison's source, appearance, and immediate effects on a victim (3-4 sentences). Make it visceral and dangerous-feeling. Include onset time.
- Traits (only ONE public):
  * Public: Primary effect (e.g., "Paralytic", "Hallucinogenic", "Lethal", "Debilitating")
  * Private: Rarity (e.g., "Common in Underdark", "Rare surface world", "Drow House secret")
  * Private: Detection difficulty (e.g., "Odorless and tasteless", "Distinct bitter smell", "Detectable by magic")

BACK OF CARD (STATS):
- Toxicity: [1-10, where 10=almost always fatal]
- Onset Time (minutes): [actual number]
- Duration (hours): [actual number if temporary, or "Permanent" or "Until cured"]
- Save DC: [12-20, for D&D 5e reference]

BACK OF CARD (DESCRIPTION):
Include:
- Full effects timeline (immediate, short-term, long-term)
- Symptoms progression
- Lethality and save information
- Source: What creature/plant/fungus is this derived from?
- **THE ANTIDOTE**: Detailed description of the cure
  * Ingredients required (specific Underdark flora/fauna)
  * Preparation method
  * Administration method
  * How quickly it works
  * Availability and cost
- Drow cultural notes: How do Drow use this? Assassination? Warfare? Rituals?
- Historical use or infamous cases

POISON VARIETY:
Include mix of effects:
- Paralytic poisons (famous Drow sleeping poison)
- Lethal toxins (quick death, slow agony)
- Hallucinogenic compounds (madness-inducing)
- Debilitating poisons (weakness, blindness, muteness)
- Magical toxins (interfere with spellcasting)
- Transformation poisons (petrification, etc.)
- Addiction-causing substances

NAMING STYLE:
- Dark, sinister, evocative
- Often references Underdark creatures/fungi (spider, mushroom, cave elements)
- May reference Drow houses or deities (Lolth-related)
- Examples: "Widow's Kiss", "Shadow Spore Extract", "Thornblade Venom", "Eternal Sleep Draught"

THEME: Dark fantasy, deadly and sophisticated, Underdark horror
ART STYLE: Could be Modern (clean, scientific botanical/alchemical style) or Classic (sinister watercolor) depending on tone preference
```

## Usage Notes

These prompts are designed to work with the AI deck generation system. Simply paste the entire prompt (including all the FRONT/BACK specifications) into the deck generation interface, adjust the card count if needed, and generate!

Each prompt is optimized for:

- Terse trait descriptions (5-10 words)
- Only ONE public trait
- Contextually appropriate stats (or no stats when not needed)
- Avoid repetition between description, traits, and stats
- Theme-appropriate content that will generate great images
